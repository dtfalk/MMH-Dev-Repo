My Mom Is Here

"Not all audiences may know jazz, but few will resist such a pleasant singing voice, and music played so smoothly."



My Mom Is Here is a New York City-based band blending the soul of jazz with the accessibility of pop and the energy of classic rock. Formed by vocalist Samantha Seiff and pianist Nick Sare, the group features Columbia jazz alumni Aidan Speckhard (drums), Stephen Park (guitar), and Ryan Daar (bass).

The band debuted in June 2024 with “Didn’t Sleep Tonight”, a soulful, groove-forward track inspired by a chaotic night out. The song was largely written by Seiff during a 6 a.m. Uber ride and built around a catchy bass riff composed by Sare—who is also a founding member of Melt the Band.

Drawing on influences like Fleetwood Mac, The Rolling Stones, Stevie Wonder, and Tedeschi Trucks Band, My Mom Is Here creates music that’s rich in texture, bold in ambition, and always danceable. Their sound wraps the expansive freedom of jazz and neo-soul in a polished, modern package.

Their second single, “Memory Jar,” is set to release in August 2024.
